## Introduction

I'm Aditya — an AI Systems Engineer working across AI product engineering, distributed systems, and full-stack development. I build production-shaped software: things with real users, real failure modes, and real maintenance costs, not just demos.

**What I build:** AI-native products and the infrastructure underneath them — from LLM-powered agents and RAG systems to the APIs, data layers, and interfaces that make them usable.

**Why I build:** I'm most interested in the gap between "a model that works in a notebook" and "a system that works in production." Closing that gap — reliability, evaluation, performance, UX — is where I spend most of my attention.

**What I believe:** good engineering is mostly about making good decisions under constraints — and being honest about what a system does and doesn't do yet.

<img src="./assets/divider.svg" width="100%" alt=""/>

## Engineering Philosophy

<table>
<tr>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

**How I think**
I start from constraints, not features — what has to be true (latency, correctness, cost) before deciding how to build it.

**How I design software**
Small, composable pieces over monoliths. Interfaces that are boring on purpose, so the interesting logic stays isolated and testable.

</td>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

**How I solve problems**
Reproduce first, understand second, fix third. I'd rather spend an hour reading logs than ten minutes guessing.

**My principles**
Reliable · Maintainable · Scalable · Well-documented · Performance-aware · Built with long-term ownership in mind, not just to ship.

</td>
</tr>
